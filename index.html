About The Server

The ITCPR private servers are maintained by the Institute for Theoretical and Computational Physics Research to support high-performance computing, code development, data storage, and remote access for authorized researchers and collaborators in theoretical and computational physics.

RAM: 64GB DDR4

Access Policy

Access is restricted to approved users. All unauthorized attempts are logged and subject to investigation.

  • Personal credentials only — sharing is prohibited.
  • Multi-factor authentication required where applicable.
  • SSH keys must be registered with the system administrator prior to access.

Acceptable Use Policy

All users agree to the following conditions:

  • Use resources solely for approved research and educational purposes.
  • Refrain from unauthorized access, data sharing, or unlawful activity.
  • Obtain prior approval before installing third-party software.
  • Maintain system stability and respect shared resource limitations.

Data & Security Policy

Users must adhere to the following data and security guidelines:

  • All activities are monitored and logged.
  • Sensitive data must be encrypted during transfer and at rest.
  • Security breaches or suspicious behavior must be reported immediately.
  • Accounts with prolonged inactivity may be disabled or removed.

Storage Policy

Users are assigned home directories with soft storage limits. Please avoid storing unnecessary large files or binaries. The server is not a backup solution. Users are responsible for their own data backups.

Scheduled Maintenance

The servers do not follow a regular maintenance schedule. However, occasional maintenance or upgrades may be scheduled as needed. In such cases, advance notice will be provided to all users via email.

Citation Policy

If this server or its resources contributed to your research, please include an acknowledgment in your publication. The following is an example of how to cite ITCPR:

We would like to thank the Institute for Theoretical and Computational Physics Research (ITCPR), Bangladesh, for providing sufficient platform, space, and resources to accommodate this study. Their support is hugely appreciated by the authors.
